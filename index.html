About Me

Hi! I'm a third-year PhD student in the School of Computer Science at CMU, advised by Prof. Jordan Lee. My research lives at the boundary between programming languages, machine learning, and cognitive science.

Before CMU, I completed my BS in CS and Mathematics at MIT, where I did undergrad research on verified compilation with Prof. Angela Park. I also spent a year as a software engineer at a startup building developer tooling, which is where I first got hooked on the question of how to make programming feel less like talking to a brick wall.

Outside of research: I'm an amateur espresso nerd ☕, a mediocre but enthusiastic climber 🧗, and I occasionally write about whatever's on my mind — usually something about AI, but sometimes just life — on my blog.
