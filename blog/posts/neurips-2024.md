# My Experience Presenting at NeurIPS 2024

Getting a paper accepted at NeurIPS was a milestone moment. But presenting it?
That was a whole different kind of challenge.

## The Lead-Up

The weeks before the conference were a whirlwind — finishing the camera-ready
version, designing the poster, and rehearsing my pitch. I must have explained
the paper to my labmates a dozen times.

## Poster Session

Standing next to my poster for three hours sounds exhausting (and it is), but
it's also incredibly rewarding. You get real-time feedback from people who
genuinely care about the topic. A few conversations led to ideas I'm still
exploring.

## Tips for First-Time Presenters

1. **Practice your 2-minute pitch.** Most people will give you about that long
   before deciding whether to stay.
2. **Make the poster visual.** Fewer words, more diagrams. People can read the
   paper later.
3. **Ask questions back.** "What's your work on?" turns a presentation into a
   conversation.
4. **Take notes after.** Jot down the interesting questions and suggestions you
   received — you *will* forget otherwise.

## Beyond the Poster

The best part of conferences is the hallway conversations, the workshops, and
the dinners. Don't just attend talks — meet people, ask questions, and be
genuinely curious.

---

If you're presenting for the first time soon — you've got this. It's
nerve-wracking but incredibly worthwhile. 💪
