# How I Organize My Research Workflow

After a year of trial and error, I've settled into a workflow that keeps me
sane. Here's how I manage the chaos of papers, experiments, and writing.

## Reading & Notes

I use **Zotero** for managing papers and **Obsidian** for interconnected
notes. Every paper I read gets a short summary note with tags. Over time, a
web of ideas emerges that's incredibly useful for literature reviews.

## Experiment Tracking

I keep every experiment in a Git repo with a clear directory structure:

```
project/
├── configs/       # Hyperparameters as YAML
├── scripts/       # Training & evaluation scripts
├── notebooks/     # Exploratory analysis
└── results/       # Logs, checkpoints, figures
```

For tracking metrics I use **Weights & Biases** — the ability to compare
runs visually has saved me countless hours.

## Writing

I write papers in **LaTeX** with Overleaf for collaboration. For blog posts
and personal notes, Markdown is king. I draft first, edit later — the worst
thing you can do is try to write a perfect first sentence.

## Daily Routine

- **Morning**: Deep work (coding, math, writing)
- **Afternoon**: Meetings, reading, lighter tasks
- **Evening**: Gym, hobbies, rest

The key insight: protect your deep work hours. Everything else can flex.
